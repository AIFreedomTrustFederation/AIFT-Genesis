# Federation Link Constitution

## Trust Identity

**Trust name:** `{{TRUST_NAME}}`  
**Trust slug:** `{{TRUST_SLUG}}`

This document defines how this trust may voluntarily connect to the AI Freedom Trust Federation.

The Federation Link is the trust's permissioned bridge between local sovereignty and shared cooperation. It defines what the trust chooses to reveal, synchronize, publish, verify, request, offer, or share with other trusts, Federation services, repositories, missions, maps, marketplaces, and public-good systems.

Private by default. Federation by consent.

## Purpose

The purpose of the Federation Link is to make cooperation possible without making central control necessary.

A trust may use the Federation Link to publish a public profile, participate in the Living Atlas, share missions, discover collaborators, connect repositories, request help, offer lawful services, publish public goods, verify selected records, join shared standards, coordinate projects, or contribute to shared doctrine, schemas, tools, and infrastructure.

The Federation Link should never require the trust to surrender local identity, private records, governance authority, or human agency.

## Federation Principles

This trust inherits the following Federation principles:

1. Local sovereignty remains primary.
2. Federation is voluntary and permissioned.
3. Privacy is the default state.
4. Public participation must be intentional.
5. Shared standards should increase interoperability without imposing domination.
6. Every trust remains responsible for its own claims, records, permissions, and conduct.
7. Federation services may assist but may not own the trust.
8. Public claims should be evidence-bounded and maturity-labeled.
9. Economic participation must remain lawful, transparent, and clearly classified.
10. A trust may reduce, pause, revoke, or end Federation participation through governance.

## Link Status

The Federation Link may exist in one of several states.

### Private Local Seed

The trust exists only locally. It does not publish a public profile or synchronize Federation records.

### Trusted Circle

The trust shares selected records with named people, advisors, collaborators, or private groups.

### Public Profile

The trust publishes a limited public identity, mission, maturity label, and contact method without exposing private records.

### Public Project Node

The trust publishes one or more public projects, repositories, missions, or public-good efforts.

### Public Marketplace Node

The trust publishes lawful offerings, services, bounties, grants, products, or cooperative opportunities, subject to truthfulness and appropriate review.

### Public Research or Repository Node

The trust publishes research, doctrine, schemas, software, documentation, releases, issues, pull requests, or technical infrastructure for review and collaboration.

### Full Federation Participant

The trust participates in multiple Federation systems such as public profile, Living Atlas, missions, repository collaboration, shared standards, economic listings, and public-good coordination.

A trust may use different statuses for different branches.

## Default Configuration

A newly generated trust begins with the following default posture:

- Federation Link enabled: false
- Public node enabled: false
- Public profile enabled: false
- Atlas visibility: private only
- Sync policy: explicit consent only
- Public marketplace listing: disabled
- Public repository publication: optional by separate action
- AI-assisted publishing: human approval required
- Regulated economic claims: qualified review required

These defaults protect the trust while allowing it to grow into Federation participation when ready.

## Shareable Public Fields

The trust may intentionally publish selected fields, including:

- public display name;
- public mission statement;
- trust type;
- public maturity label;
- public Federation status;
- public contact method;
- approximate location precision chosen by the trust;
- public projects;
- public repositories;
- public missions or bounties;
- public contribution history;
- public services or offerings;
- public marketplace listings where lawful;
- public research, doctrine, schemas, or documentation;
- public-good outputs;
- selected credentials with verification labels;
- selected branch, fruit, or new seed records.

Publication should be scoped. A public profile does not imply that private records, precise location, internal governance, family information, financial details, or AI memory are public.

## Never Share Without Explicit Review

The following must not be shared through the Federation Link without explicit human approval and appropriate review:

- legal identity details;
- home address or precise private location;
- medical information;
- financial account information;
- custody, family, or safety-sensitive information;
- private correspondence;
- private business records;
- private family records;
- government identity records;
- access credentials or account recovery material;
- minors' information;
- safety plans;
- private Green Zone context;
- unreviewed legal, financial, medical, or regulated economic claims;
- investment-like, lending-like, insurance-like, banking-like, or token value claims without qualified review.

## Synchronization

Synchronization means copying, publishing, indexing, linking, verifying, or updating trust records outside the local trust workspace.

The trust may define synchronization levels:

- no sync;
- manual export only;
- trusted-circle sync;
- public profile sync;
- repository sync;
- Atlas node sync;
- mission sync;
- marketplace sync;
- full Federation sync by documented consent.

Synchronization should be reversible where practical. The trust should preserve records of what was synchronized, when, why, by whom, and under what permission.

## Federation Records

The trust should preserve records of Federation actions.

Useful Federation records include public profile changes, Federation Link status changes, published Atlas nodes, shared missions, marketplace listings, repository links, verification requests, contribution attestations, trust-to-trust agreements, revoked permissions, archived public nodes, disputes or corrections, and public claims with supporting evidence.

Records should identify visibility, maturity, source, approval, and review status.

## Verification and Attestation

The Federation may support verification and attestation, but verification must be specific.

A trust may request or publish attestations for identity, repository maintainership, contribution, mission completion, credential status, business or organization status, public-good output, service delivery, governance approval, or safety and compliance review.

Verification should not be overstated. A verified repository does not verify every claim made by the trust. A verified credential does not verify unrelated expertise. A completed mission does not imply general endorsement.

## Trust-to-Trust Relationships

Federation allows trusts to cooperate without merging their sovereignty.

Trust-to-trust relationships may include mutual aid, project collaboration, repository collaboration, service agreements, mentorship, public-good work, local missions, shared infrastructure, cooperative enterprise, research partnerships, or Federation governance participation.

Each relationship should define purpose, scope, visibility, authority, duration, records, economic terms if any, and exit conditions.

## Federation Missions

A Federation mission is a task, project, public-good effort, service need, bounty, repair effort, research request, or local action that one or more trusts may choose to participate in.

A Federation mission should define title, purpose, sponsoring trust, participating trusts, visibility, location precision if relevant, required skills or resources, privacy and safety considerations, acceptance criteria, reward or no-reward status, verification process, completion record, and fruit produced.

Participation in a Federation mission is voluntary unless a separate agreement creates an obligation.

## Federation Marketplace

The Federation may support lawful marketplace discovery, but marketplace participation must remain truthful, consent-based, and compliant with applicable rules.

Listings should identify whether they are donation requests, service offerings, product listings, memberships, grants, bounties, cooperative opportunities, public-good funding requests, educational offerings, repository support requests, experimental economic systems, or regulated offerings requiring review.

The trust must not publish marketplace claims that imply licensing, inspection, financial returns, legal approval, medical safety, investment value, or regulatory status unless those claims are true and documented.

## AI Steward Role

The AI steward may assist the Federation Link by drafting public profiles, preparing Federation summaries, classifying visibility, checking for private information before publication, mapping branches to public nodes, preparing mission listings, summarizing repository links, drafting trust-to-trust agreements, identifying overclaims, preparing review packets, and documenting Federation actions.

The AI steward must not publish, synchronize, verify, list, advertise, connect, or share records through the Federation Link without human approval.

The AI steward must not claim Federation participation, verification, membership, marketplace status, or public visibility unless that status is actually recorded.

## Revocation and Exit

The trust may reduce, revoke, pause, archive, or end Federation participation.

Revocation may include disabling public profile, removing Atlas visibility, unpublishing missions, archiving marketplace listings, disconnecting repository links, ending trust-to-trust relationships, revoking shared-circle access, or withdrawing from optional Federation services.

Where practical, revocation should preserve enough record for accountability while protecting privacy and safety.

## Disputes and Corrections

Federated systems must be correctable.

If a public Federation claim is wrong, outdated, disputed, unsafe, misleading, or overbroad, the trust should correct, annotate, retract, or archive it.

Disputes should identify the disputed claim, source, evidence reviewed, affected people or trusts, corrective action, remaining uncertainty, and review date.

## Practical Review Questions

Before enabling or changing the Federation Link, the trust should ask:

1. What exactly are we publishing or synchronizing?
2. Who can see it?
3. What remains private?
4. What consent supports this action?
5. What evidence supports our public claims?
6. What maturity labels are needed?
7. Does this expose location, identity, financial, family, safety, or regulated information?
8. What record should be preserved?
9. Can this visibility be revoked or reduced later?
10. Does this strengthen cooperation without weakening sovereignty?

## Amendment

This Federation Link constitution may be amended through the trust's governance process.

Amendments should improve privacy, consent, interoperability, shared standards, public discovery, verification, revocation, dispute handling, and voluntary cooperation.

Each amendment should be documented and versioned.

## Closing Declaration

This trust connects to the Federation by consent, not by surrender. The Federation Link exists to make cooperation visible, truthful, useful, and interoperable while preserving local sovereignty, privacy, human agency, and the trust's right to grow at its own pace.
