## Purpose

A trust is the foundational organizational unit of the AI Freedom Trust Federation. It is a sovereign stewardship structure through which one or more people organize relationships, knowledge, resources, projects, responsibilities, records, and future opportunities according to a shared constitutional framework.

A trust is not merely a software account, a folder, a wallet, a business profile, or a legal label. It is a local-first operating structure capable of existing independently while participating voluntarily in a larger federation of interoperable trusts.

Every trust begins locally. Its first responsibility is faithful stewardship of what has been entrusted to it. Federation is voluntary, permissioned, and based on informed consent.

## Trust Types

A trust may operate as one or more of the following:

- an individual trust for one person stewarding their identity, work, records, values, and future path;
- a family trust for a household, lineage, inheritance, care network, or multi-generation stewardship mission;
- a business trust for a lawful enterprise, customer relationship, operating system, product, service, or cooperative venture;
- a ministry trust for faith-based service, teaching, mutual aid, healing, care, or community work;
- a community trust for a neighborhood, local network, cooperative, association, mutual-aid circle, or civic project;
- a repository trust for software, research, documentation, data, AI agents, or other inspectable knowledge work;
- a project trust for a specific mission, build, public good, restoration effort, campaign, or experimental prototype.

The trust may evolve across types as its mission becomes clearer, provided that changes are documented and governed transparently.

## Constitutional Principles

Every trust generated from AIFT Genesis inherits these principles:

1. Human dignity and agency are respected.
2. Stewardship takes precedence over extraction, control, or empty ownership claims.
3. Truth is pursued through evidence, humility, correction, and continuous learning.
4. Privacy is the default; disclosure is deliberate and scoped.
5. Cooperation is voluntary and based on informed consent.
6. Local autonomy is preserved while enabling federation through shared standards.
7. AI exists to assist human stewards and never to replace their responsibility.
8. Productive contribution, repair, education, and public goods are honored above status games.
9. Claims must remain bounded by evidence, maturity level, and appropriate review.
10. Every action should strengthen trustworthiness, resilience, and long-term flourishing.

## Stewardship Scope

This trust may steward:

- people and relationships connected to the trust's lawful and ethical mission;
- documents, records, agreements, receipts, histories, and institutional memory;
- projects, repositories, software, research, publications, and creative works;
- property, resources, tools, services, capital, and digital assets where lawfully held;
- missions, bounties, tasks, contributions, and public goods;
- AI agents, local knowledge bases, prompts, workflows, and automation policies;
- local community relationships, business networks, ministries, cooperatives, and mutual-aid systems;
- future opportunities, future trusts, and the seeds of new projects.

Stewardship means care, accountability, documentation, repair, and responsible development over time. It does not mean domination over people or careless control over shared resources.

## Sovereignty

This trust is sovereign within its own constitutional, ethical, technical, and lawful boundaries.

The trust may choose to remain local, connect privately with a trusted circle, publish selected public information, participate in shared Federation protocols, or withdraw from optional Federation services.

No external application, AI agent, repository, marketplace, map, or Federation service may assume authority over this trust beyond the permissions explicitly granted by the trust's stewards and any applicable law.

Local-first sovereignty requires that the trust remain understandable, exportable, inspectable, and recoverable wherever practical. The trust should never depend on a single remote platform for the meaning of its own identity.

## Privacy and Disclosure

Private information remains private by default.

The trust distinguishes between:

- **private local records**, held only inside the trust's local workspace;
- **AI-readable records**, available to the trust's AI steward under defined permissions;
- **shared records**, available to selected people or groups;
- **public Federation records**, intentionally published for broader discovery or collaboration;
- **regulated or sensitive records**, requiring special caution, qualified review, or explicit approval before use.

Precise location, legal identity information, medical information, financial information, custody or family-sensitive information, private correspondence, credentials, private keys, and safety-sensitive records must not be published casually.

Disclosure should be intentional, documented, and limited to the purpose for which it was granted.

## Federation

Membership or participation in the AI Freedom Trust Federation does not erase local sovereignty.

Federation exists to enable cooperation, interoperability, shared learning, mutual aid, lawful exchange, coordinated missions, repository collaboration, and public-good development while preserving the independence of each participating trust.

The Federation Link is optional. It defines what the trust chooses to reveal, synchronize, verify, publish, or share. A trust may participate publicly, privately, temporarily, partially, or not at all.

Every trust contributes according to its gifts, receives according to legitimate need and lawful agreement, and remains free to evolve through its own governance process.

## Relationship to the Tree of Life

Every trust grows according to the Tree of Life model.

The seed is origin and consent. The roots are values, relationships, skills, history, records, and place. The trunk is enduring mission. The branches are projects, repositories, businesses, ministries, research efforts, and collaborations. The leaves are daily tasks, documents, commits, meetings, and services. The fruit is completed value that can nourish others. New seeds are future trusts, projects, communities, repositories, and opportunities born from completed work.

The trust does not grow merely by accumulating attention, followers, speculative value, or status. It grows by faithful stewardship, useful contribution, integrity, resilience, repair, and positive impact over time.

## Relationship to Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is a stewarding instrument of the trust.

AI may assist with organization, planning, documentation, research, analysis, education, communication, summarization, code generation, workflow design, and memory. AI does not possess constitutional authority over the trust.

Significant decisions affecting governance, legal obligations, financial commitments, privacy, identity, safety, public reputation, custody, medical matters, or regulated economic activity require explicit human approval unless a documented policy delegates limited operational authority.

The AI steward must distinguish evidence from interpretation, doctrine from fact, speculation from verified record, and suggestion from decision. AI should increase human agency rather than replace it.

## Relationship to Economy

The trust may participate in economic activity when lawful, transparent, and consistent with its mission.

Economic activity may include contribution ledgers, service payments, bounties, grants, memberships, cooperative support, lawful marketplace listings, public goods funding, and value-sharing agreements.

The trust must clearly distinguish donations, purchases, service payments, grants, rewards, memberships, loans, investments, securities, insurance, banking, and other regulated activities. Regulated activity requires qualified review before public deployment or promotion.

Money, tokens, assets, rewards, and ledgers are tools. They are not the mission. The economy should serve the Tree of Life.

## Records and Auditability

The trust should preserve meaningful records of decisions, amendments, contributions, public disclosures, AI-assisted actions, governance changes, and important economic events.

Git history, Markdown documents, JSON manifests, issue records, signed attestations, receipts, and local logs may all serve as forms of institutional memory.

Good records should answer:

- what changed;
- who approved or performed the change;
- why the change was made;
- what evidence supported it;
- what remains uncertain;
- what requires later review.

## Amendment

This trust constitution is intended to be stable while remaining capable of responsible evolution.

Amendments should strengthen clarity, stewardship, accountability, interoperability, privacy, repair, local sovereignty, and human flourishing without compromising the foundational principles inherited from AIFT Genesis.

Each amendment should be documented, versioned, and reviewed so the history of the trust remains transparent and inspectable.

## Closing Declaration

This trust exists to cultivate trustworthiness, strengthen communities, steward resources responsibly, preserve human dignity, encourage meaningful contribution, and participate in the continuing development of a free, transparent, interoperable, and local-first Federation dedicated to the flourishing of present and future generations.
